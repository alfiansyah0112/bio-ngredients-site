From field to bag

Five stages. No blind spots.

Every ingredient we ship has passed through the same five hands: the agronomist, the farmer, the harvester, the miller, the QA team. Here is what each of them actually does.

01

Plan the rotation

Three years before a single kilo ships, our agronomy team sits with each partner farm to design a crop rotation that builds soil biology. Cover crops, legume breaks, and minimal-till passes are mapped out season by season. No monoculture, ever.

02

Grow with restraint

We fund the crop at planting — not on delivery — so farmers are never pushed to squeeze more out of a tired field. No synthetic fertiliser, no glyphosate, no harvest-hastening desiccants. The crop ripens on its own clock.

03

Harvest at peak

A field-level moisture and maturity check decides the harvest day — not the mill's calendar. Crops travel less than 150 km to our cleaning and drying station, where they are rested at low temperature before any further processing.

04

Process gently

Cold expeller-pressing, stone milling, and low-temperature drying, always below 45 °C. We refuse hexane, refuse high-shear, and refuse bleaching. What leaves our mill is recognisably the plant it came from.

05

Bag, lot-code, ship

Every finished bag is QA-sampled, heat-sealed under nitrogen, and stamped with a QR-linked lot passport. Open the QR and you see the exact field, operator, harvest date, moisture reading, and press temperature of that specific bag.

The promises we make

What we'll never do, in writing.

A process is only as good as the lines you refuse to cross. These are ours.

No solvent extraction

Never hexane, never isopropanol, never a chemical shortcut to squeeze a half-percent more yield from a crop.

No anonymous blending

Each lot is one field, one harvest window, one variety. We'd rather ship less than blur the origin story.

No farmer left exposed

We pre-fund the crop at planting and pay on a fixed schedule, not on spot price. Partner farms know what the harvest is worth the day they plant it.

Visit a partner farm

Come see a harvest yourself.

We host food makers at partner farms twice a year, at planting and at harvest. Three days, one crop, a lot of honest conversation.
